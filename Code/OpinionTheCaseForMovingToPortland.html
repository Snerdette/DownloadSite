Opinion Piece:

The Case for Moving to Portland

By Theo Levine

Every “Move to Portland” article hits on the same keynotes. There are in depth accolades of Portland’s many food options, including cheap and diverse food cart clusters downtown. The craft brew capitol of the world hosts dozens of breweries for beer enthusiasts. Coffee shops litter the streets. There are endless concerts, sporting events and other happenings for those seeking entertainment.

For some Catalyst employees, the draw to move the office downtown is captured by Portland’s amenities. For others, happy hours and six dollar pad thai just don’t raise the needle. Some feel more comfortable with the chain restaurants and office parks of Beaverton. While the charm of Beaverton may hold sway over some of us, the prospect of being a tech company in Downtown Portland is something that should appeal to all of us.

Catalyst moving downtown would be an instant boost of exposure. Portland’s rising tech companies are all there. Whereas in the west suburbs monolithic companies Nike and Intel dominate the business environment, in Portland a slew of tech startups have made a name for themselves. Being a part of the tech scene means better exposure, and better exposure means more business.

The Beaverton office moving to a location that will generate more business is something that will benefit everyone. Moving to Portland will cost more money in overhead, but the returns from increased sales and attracting new clients could more than balance that out. Higher utilization rates means more promotions and raises – money that goes right into employees’ pockets.

Employees will benefit beyond what Catalyst gains in a move to Portland. Portland is an environment flush with networking opportunities, giving employees a chance to make contacts and grow their careers. Tech conferences happening downtown would suddenly be a short walk from the office, giving employees more flexibility to expand their knowledge base as well as spread word of Catalyst’s work.

For some members of our community Portland can seem like a scary place. There are lots of people, and parking can be hard (the MAX isn’t so bad if you bring a book). There can be many excuses made for why to not move downtown, but in the end there are opportunities there for both the company and the employees.

Also, Pokémon Go in downtown Portland is lit.
