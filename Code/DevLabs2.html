DevLabs 2.0

DevLabs Enhancement Project

By Dave Kaus

A major goal of DevLabs is to provide Catalyst employees with opportunities to gain experience working on high-functioning project teams that operate with the expectations and standards we have for client projects. Teams learn how Catalyst expects projects to be run in a safe environment, without the potential of damaging client relationships. Now all this sounds great, but how do we expect our teams to know how to conduct successful projects? What documentation needs to be created before Sprint 0? Who exactly needs to be involved in specific meetings? What decisions need to be made by certain dates?

It’s extremely important that we have the resources and oversight put in place in order to ensure that our teams know how Catalyst wants projects to be run. To help accomplish this, we are making a few improvements to DevLabs.

Revised Project Walkthrough
While browsing the DevLabs Confluence Page, you may have noticed the Project Walkthrough section. This step-by-step guide outlines what should be done after a DevLabs project has been approved (think of this as DevLab’s version of the Software Delivery Framework). While this is a huge step in the right direction, our current walkthrough could use some TLC. In order to make the walkthrough more effective, we are fleshing out each section and presenting the information in a more digestible format. Additionally, we are making sure the DevLabs process adheres as closely as possible to the Software Delivery Framework. After reading this you may be asking yourself, “well how will we know if we are applying the walkthrough correctly and up to Catalyst’s standards?” Keep reading and you’ll find out!

Development, Engagement and Architectural Oversight
Moving forward, every new DevLabs project will have development, engagement and architectural oversight. Individuals in these oversight roles will be engagement managers and senior developers with deep client experience and a solid understanding of how successful projects are run. The plan is that those providing oversight will closely monitor DevLabs teams (more so at the beginning of projects) in order to provide ongoing feedback on performance and delivery. This is especially valuable for our more junior team members that do not have a lot of experience working on projects or leading teams.

If you would like to know more about the DevLabs Enhancement Project, or you are interested in participating, please contact David Kaus
