Building Personal Brand with LinkedIn

By Marty Brandwin

LinkedIn is one of the oldest social networks, and boasts 433 million users. The numbers are proof that most of us, if not all of us, have a LinkedIn profile. LinkedIn is a social network with a strong business slant. This social network presents us with a forum to highlight our skills, accomplishments and career successes. It allows us to connect with colleagues and mentors and establish our own personal brand.

Over the course of my career, as a hiring manager, I have seen LinkedIn under-utilized and have seen personal brands poorly developed. As a marketing professional, I am a firm believer that company brand is tied to every contact/communication point with customers, prospects and fellow employees. Personal brand influences company brand and vice versa.

For both Catalyst and your own career, your personal brand is important. To make your profile more noticeable, to differentiate yourself, and to better engage other LinkedIn members, consider the following:

Authenticity
Your profile should be a genuine representation of your personality. That means your identity is real, honest and accurate. Your profile should focus on what you do and what you offer to your company. There is no need for a sales spin when you focus on your unique capabilities and value.

Visibility
Strive for visibility. Create a distinct headline for your profile that resonates with other members. Make your headline true to your skills, your focus and your unique value. Avoid cliché statements like “team player” which carry little weight and are grossly overused.

Accuracy
Keep your profile updated. LinkedIn members want to know what you do. For each employer, tell what you did and why it was valuable. Don’t just list a position – be specific, share the fact that you managed a team, increased the success of a business, improved customer service, etc. Highlight the value and skills you developed in each position and, where possible, share metrics to illustrate your success.

Feature
You work hard on countless projects. Enhance your profile and create projects to highlight the work you are doing for your employer. Once the project is complete, add a link to the project if possible. Associating your profile with the work you are doing illustrates your capabilities. And, by adding your projects, you help reinforce the Catalyst brand and the value our teams contribute to enterprises around the country.

Engage
This is true for all social networks, but with LinkedIn it’s imperative to connect, follow and share. Connect with your colleagues. Follow the organizations that are important to your career – your employer, the companies you do work for, and thought leaders in topics of interest to you.

When a company or person you follow publishes an update or post, share it (and bonus, by sharing Catalyst’s daily updates you also help to drive awareness of Catalyst). Make yourself a resource for interesting posts/information, and you’ll see your connections and follows grow. Join LinkedIn Groups and participate with these communities to help market yourself and build credibility.

Personalize
When you request to connect with someone, personalize that request. Generic requests are often ignored, or worse they’re seen as spam. Briefly note why you want to connect and how you found them.

LinkedIn can be a powerful tool if used correctly. Whether you’re using it to stay in touch with old colleagues or to learn more valuable information to help your career, it’s a great network. Remember to always be honest and authentic in your communications, create projects to share the work you are doing (and associate those projects with your Catalyst employment), be consistent in the message/value you communicate, and be active – share others’ posts, congratulate others in your network on their accomplishments, offer advice in groups, ask questions and respond to others. And finally, watch your personal brand and influence grow.
