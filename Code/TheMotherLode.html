The Mother Lode

by Scott Harris

As we endure the summer heat wave in the East, our client development efforts continue to heat up as well. So much to talk about with so little space. Let me start by telling you that we continue to evangelize to CIO/CTO’s and their “lieutenants” on how we can help them evolve their organizations to support the digital transformation of their business, be more responsive to changing market conditions, and re-envision how they engage with customers. For companies like Cambia, that means competing in non-traditional areas like prevention, wellness, and fitness and against newcomers like ZoomCare (another growing Catalyst customer). For Choice and Vistana that means competing against non-traditional companies like Airbnb in the shared economy.

SO how has this been translating into business? Good question! Our message is being well-received, resulting in the growth of our pipeline of opportunities with meaningful companies like American Automobile Association (Orlando) and REI (Seattle). We’re progressing through contracts with Deloitte (Chicago) and Freedom Mortgage (NJ), both of whom are doing interesting innovative work which we’ll highlight in an upcoming issue of The Download.

In addition to building a more robust pipeline we have closed some new customers and new projects since the last Download. To highlight a few:

Gartner:
You have all heard Mike and Marty talk about our relationship with the leading technology industry analyst group, Gartner. We talk to their analysts regularly about Catalyst and they in turn talk to technology executives about us as a leader. Completely independent of our analyst relationship, Gartner’s internal technology group has engaged Catalyst to help with their evolution and adoption of agile, as well as to help them to accelerate delivery of enhancements to a critical business. Along with agile coaching/mentoring, a Catalyst team will be adding important enhancements to the Gartner content platform and publishing system. This is the system their analysts use to author, collaborate, publish and consume content. It’s one of the most strategic systems they have and the foundation of what makes them a leader in the market.

Cockram Scenario:
This new customer came in as a result of a professional relationship of Paolo Hilario (nice job Paolo). Cockram Scenario is a global construction firm specializing in world class attraction and theme park construction (they build fake rocks better than anyone else). Catalyst is helping them automate the design and construction process of the rockwork for theme park scenes and rides. We will be building automated solutions on top of Autodesk design software including AutoCAD and 3D Studio Max.

Inovalon:
One of the newer companies in the healthcare market, Inovalon focuses on the use of analytics to help practitioners provide better care to individuals. Our initial involvement will be on site in Atlanta where we’ll be working with their next gen platform team to build front-ends (in React.js) that provide medical professionals access to patient data/profiles to support decisions relating to patient diagnoses and care.

Cake Systems:
Born out of a professional relationship of Lisa Slaven’s, Cake Systems provides a comprehensive software platform for managing energy efficiency programs that support various stakeholder groups such as homeowners, program managers and contractors. On a tight timeline, Catalyst will be responsible for accelerating major enhancements to their core platform that are required to enable strategic business opportunities for the growth of the company.
